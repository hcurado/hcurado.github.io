45-MINUTE SESSIONS

Do you consider yourself a “sweets” person, a “salty” person or an ‘anything’ person? Many of us have food cravings that overpower our days, which typically show up at 3:00 in the afternoon or in the evenings after a long day. These cravings can happen for various reasons, including emotional triggers such as lack of balance in your day, as a reward from busy/stressful situations or not having enough time for yourself to name a few.

When I was in the process of losing weight, I was eating a low fat diet and in turn would constantly crave sugar. I found myself eating something sweet for a snack in the middle of the morning, with lunch, at 3:00 as a ‘pick-me-up’ and again after dinner. What I didn’t know at the time was that one of the things I was lacking in my diet was good fat. As soon as I added more good fats into my meals, my sugar cravings subsided. This is just one example of how cravings can show up when balance is missing from your routine.

During these sessions you and Laura will work together to address making small habit changes while establishing balance without deprivation. Laura will also identify some of the external factors that may be causing some of your cravings because once you’re aware of them; the pull to food can be released.
